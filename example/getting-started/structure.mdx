## Folders become sections

Every folder under your `MDX` directory becomes a sidebar section, and every `.mdx` file inside it
becomes a page. Nesting works to any depth.

By default, sections and pages are ordered **alphabetically**, and their names are derived from the
slug — `getting-started` becomes "getting started".

## Overriding the order

Alphabetical is rarely the order you want to *read* things in. Two optional settings fix that,
without moving any files.

`NAV_ORDER` sets the order of folders. Anything you leave out keeps the default order, at the end:

```yaml
nav_order: |
  getting-started
  writing
```

You can also group folders under a heading:

```yaml
nav_order: |
  Basics:
    - getting-started
  Authoring:
    - writing
```

`NAV_LABELS` fixes the names that automatic capitalization gets wrong:

```yaml
nav_labels: |
  sql: SQL
  getting-started: Getting Started
```

## Ordering pages within a section

Inside a folder, the `nav` key in a page's frontmatter sets its position:

```mdx
---
title: Introduction
nav: 0
---
```

Lower numbers come first. Pages without `nav` fall back to alphabetical order.
